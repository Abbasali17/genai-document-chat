The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star (G2V) located at the center of our Solar System. It accounts for about 99.86% of the total mass of the Solar System. Light from the Sun takes approximately 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to the iron oxide prevalent on its surface, which gives it a reddish appearance. It has the largest volcano and the deepest, longest canyon in our solar system, Valles Marineris. Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos.

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined. Jupiter is known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm larger than Earth.
